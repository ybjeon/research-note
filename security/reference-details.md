# Reference details

## Security Engineering - Ross Anderson

### Part I. Core Foundations
- Chapter 1. What Is Security Engineering? - introduces the scope and core problems of security engineering.
- Chapter 2. Who Is the Opponent? - covers attacker models and sources of threats.
- Chapter 3. Psychology and Usability - explains how usability, human behavior, mistakes, and deception affect security.
- Chapter 4. Protocols - examines security protocol design and failure patterns.
- Chapter 5. Cryptography - summarizes the basic concepts and practical use of cryptography.
- Chapter 6. Access Control - covers authorization, authentication, and the structure of access control.
- Chapter 7. Distributed Systems - explains trust, failures, and attack surfaces in distributed environments.
- Chapter 8. Economics - discusses the economic incentives that shape security decisions.

### Part II. Specialized Security Domains
- Chapter 9. Multilevel Security - explains classification-based security and policy separation.
- Chapter 10. Boundaries - covers trust boundaries and system boundary management.
- Chapter 11. Inference Control - summarizes ways to prevent unintended disclosure through inference.
- Chapter 12. Banking and Bookkeeping - examines security in financial systems and record integrity.
- Chapter 13. Locks and Alarms - discusses physical security devices and intrusion detection.
- Chapter 14. Monitoring and Metering - explains the security and misuse risks of monitoring and metering systems.
- Chapter 15. Nuclear Command and Control - covers command-and-control security where extremely high assurance is required.
- Chapter 16. Security Printing and Seals - summarizes security printing, seals, and anti-forgery techniques.
- Chapter 17. Biometrics - explains the strengths, limits, and attack potential of biometrics.
- Chapter 18. Tamper Resistance - covers tamper resistance and hardware protection techniques.
- Chapter 19. Side Channels - explains side-channel attacks such as timing, power, and radiation leakage.
- Chapter 20. Advanced Cryptographic Engineering - summarizes advanced cryptographic engineering from an implementation perspective.
- Chapter 21. Network Attack and Defence - covers major network attack and defense patterns.
- Chapter 22. Phones - examines security issues in telephony and communication devices.
- Chapter 23. Electronic and Information Warfare - discusses the security aspects of electronic and information warfare.
- Chapter 24. Copyright and DRM - covers the technical and policy issues around copyright protection and DRM.
- Chapter 25. New Directions? - reviews emerging research topics and future directions in security.

### Part III. Broader Societal and Engineering Issues
- Chapter 26. Surveillance or Privacy? - discusses the balance between surveillance and privacy.
- Chapter 27. Secure Systems Development - summarizes the practice and process of building secure systems.
- Chapter 28. Assurance and Sustainability - explains assurance, validation, and long-term maintainability.
- Chapter 29. Beyond “Computer Says No” - discusses security limits across policy, organizations, and social context.

## Threat Modeling - Adam Shostack

### Part I. Getting Started
- Chapter 1. Dive In and Threat Model! - introduces how to get started with threat modeling and the basic mindset.
- Chapter 2. Strategies for Threat Modeling - summarizes strategies and approaches for threat modeling.

### Part II. Finding Threats
- Chapter 3. STRIDE - explains the STRIDE threat classification model.
- Chapter 4. Attack Trees - covers how to analyze attack goals as tree structures.
- Chapter 5. Attack Libraries - summarizes reusable attack knowledge and patterns.
- Chapter 6. Privacy Tools - introduces tools and techniques for privacy-focused analysis.

### Part III. Managing and Addressing Threats
- Chapter 7. Processing and Managing Threats - covers how to classify, prioritize, and track identified threats.
- Chapter 8. Defensive Tactics and Technologies - summarizes defensive tactics and technical countermeasures.
- Chapter 9. Trade-Offs When Addressing Threats - explains the trade-offs among security, cost, and usability.
- Chapter 10. Validating That Threats Are Addressed - covers how to verify that countermeasures are actually sufficient.
- Chapter 11. Threat Modeling Tools - introduces tools and workflows that support threat modeling.

### Part IV. Threat Modeling in Technologies and Tricky Areas
- Chapter 12. Requirements Cookbook - summarizes practical guidance for bringing threats into requirements work.
- Chapter 13. Web and Cloud Threats - covers common threats in web and cloud environments.
- Chapter 14. Accounts and Identity - explains threats around accounts, authentication, and identity management.
- Chapter 15. Human Factors and Usability - connects people and usability issues to threat modeling.
- Chapter 16. Threats to Cryptosystems - covers threats to cryptosystems and common implementation mistakes.

### Part V. Taking It to the Next Level
- Chapter 17. Bringing Threat Modeling to Your Organization - explains how to scale threat modeling across an organization.
- Chapter 18. Experimental Approaches - explores experimental methods and data-driven approaches.
- Chapter 19. Architecting for Success - summarizes the architectural and operational conditions that make threat modeling work well.
